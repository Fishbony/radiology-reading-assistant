# Body-wide Radiology Interpretation Checklist

## Universal checklist

- Confirm modality, region, projection/plane, and sequence
- Assess image quality and completeness
- Review urgent findings first
- Localize the abnormality anatomically
- Describe morphology, signal/density, enhancement, diffusion, calcification, fat, hemorrhage, gas, and necrosis
- Check secondary signs and complications
- Compare with prior studies if available
- Provide differential diagnosis with supporting and opposing features
- State confidence and limitations
- Suggest next imaging or clinical correlation only when useful

## Critical findings checklist

- Airway compromise
- Tension pneumothorax
- Aortic dissection/rupture
- Pulmonary embolism with right-heart strain
- Active hemorrhage
- Bowel perforation/ischemia/closed-loop obstruction
- Intracranial hemorrhage/herniation
- Acute stroke/LVO signs
- Spinal cord or cauda equina compression
- Necrotizing infection
- Unstable fracture/dislocation
