# Correction Example: Fibrous Dysplasia vs Pitt Pit in the Proximal Femur

This de-identified correction note records a common pitfall in proximal femur lesion interpretation.

```text
Correction note:
- Initial impression: Pitt pit / synovial herniation pit at the femoral head-neck junction.
- Final diagnosis: Fibrous dysplasia of the proximal femur.
- Evidence for final diagnosis: User-provided final diagnosis / teaching case correction.
- Missed or underweighted imaging clue: The lesion should not be judged only by proximity to the femoral head-neck junction. Assess whether it is intramedullary, has ground-glass matrix, mild expansion, cortical thinning, or proximal femoral neck involvement.
- Mimic/pitfall: Small femoral neck lucency with sclerosis may suggest Pitt pit, but fibrous dysplasia can involve the proximal femur/femoral neck and appear as a lucent or ground-glass lesion.
- Rule for next time: For proximal femur lesions, explicitly separate small subcortical Pitt pit from intramedullary fibrous dysplasia. If ground-glass matrix, expansile remodeling, cortical thinning, or broader medullary involvement is present, prioritize fibrous dysplasia.
- Suggested literature/reference to add: Reviews or teaching references on fibrous dysplasia imaging and benign bone tumor mimics.
```
